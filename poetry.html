Under my skin

I wish my skin was a jacket I could just zip open after a long
day of work and toss carelessly to the edge
of my bed as I snuggled the rest
of me into my blanket. That way, I could choose
a new skin to wear each day. I could discard my old,
bruised skins without a worry, and begin a new day
as a new me.

Or what if I had no skin at all?
Like a peeled banana.
I could walk around town a bloody, mushy lump of flesh,
concealing thin tubes pumping
red fluid into every crevasse
of my body. And everyone would be
too scared to touch me.

Self-Diagnosis

Clouds of imagination condense and
precipitate in my head. Gravity tugs the
creative droplets downwards and they trickle
down the back of my throat and meander
across the curve of my right shoulder. They
drip through my veins and accelerate towards
the tip of my fingers and then they
stop. My scorching skin boils the droplets
until they evaporate. And then just like that,
they dissipate. The piece of paper is as dry as
it was when I first picked it up. I think
I am broken.

red fluid into every crevasse
of my body. And everyone would be
too scared to touch me.

Love Poem

Love is a mug of hot chocolate
on a cold and stormy night.
Its warmth swaddles you like
a mother wrapping her
new-born baby in her gentle embrace.
Its sweetness is euphoric,
protecting you from the thunderous
rain outside. But sometimes it can be
sickening. And scorch your upper lip
as you try to take a sip. And if you don’t
take care as you place the mug
down on your bedside table, it may
spill all over. Or worse, shatter,
leaving you to pick up the jagged shards. All alone.

Mr Medusa

You had a magic touch.
You’d twirl and snap and prod
until I turned into stone. Then you’d
chisel me away, chip by chip, until I was
smoothed to your liking. Once you were done
fiddling, you’d toss me away and then I’d
shatter into jagged shards, hurting none other but
myself. At night, I’d glue myself back together with my tears.

Mother Knows Best

you fell from the sky
trapped in a stone hard shell
that grimy old thing
i’m sure you don’t miss it one bit
it was sealed shut
but my tears cracked it open
and then a thousand lights burst through
yellow and sparkling like your hair
you see that sun?
it shines for you

your little heart was broken
black like coal and fading by the second
you poor thing
aren’t you glad i found you
your skin was scaly and peeling
your blood dark and seething
but i whisked you away
inside where it is safe and cosy
and then your heart turned gold
and magic filled your veins
you see this house?
its built for you

you couldn’t stop crying
your shriek could deafen the wildest crow
you lucky girl
you were in so much pain weren’t you
your throat was throbbing
and your eyes were flooding
but then i sang you a sweet bird’s song
and then i wrapped you in my arms
you held onto me for your dear life
and asked me to never let go
you hear my heart?
it beats for you
